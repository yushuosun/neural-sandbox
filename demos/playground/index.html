How it works

Every point you draw is a training example in the square [-1,1]² with a label (class A = +1, class B = -1). A small multilayer perceptron, implemented from scratch in plain JavaScript, runs full-batch gradient descent: forward pass → mean-squared loss against a tanh output → manual backpropagation → weight update. Gradients are clamped to stay numerically stable. After each batch of steps the network is evaluated on a grid and the result is painted as a smooth decision-surface heatmap behind your points.

Why it matters

It makes the abstract concrete: you feel how learning rate, depth, and activation reshape the decision boundary in real time, and why some layouts (XOR, spirals) demand non-linear capacity. It is the "you draw the data" cousin of TensorFlow Playground — a hands-on intuition pump for how neural networks carve up space.
